Curriculum

What You Will Master

Six progressive modules moving from research philosophy through AI-augmented analysis. Each module connects theory to hands-on practice.

MODULE 01
🧭
Foundations & Philosophy
Ontology, epistemology, positivism, and where quantitative research sits in the AI era. Building a defensible worldview.
PHILOSOPHY · PARADIGMS
MODULE 02
📊
Descriptive & Inferential Statistics
The four moments. Normal distributions, chi-square, t-tests, correlation, and regression — with AI interpretation layers.
SPSS · PYTHON · R
MODULE 03
📐
Measurement & Scales
Validity, reliability, Cronbach's alpha, CFA. Designing instruments that hold up to rigorous scrutiny.
RELIABILITY · VALIDITY
MODULE 04
🔗
Structural Equation Modelling
PLS-SEM with SmartPLS v3. Path analysis, CFA, mediation, moderation, and multi-group analysis.
SMARTPLS · AMOS · SEM
MODULE 05
🤖
AI & Machine Learning in Research
When to use ML vs. SEM. Regression forests, NLP for survey analysis, AI-assisted literature review. The frontier methods.
PYTHON · ML · NLP
MODULE 06
✍️
Writing & Reporting Results
APA tables, interpreting outputs, framing contributions. How to write results that reviewers and examiners cannot fault.
APA · DISSERTATION

About

Your Instructor

TK
Dr. Teik-Kheong Tan
PhD · IEEE · Stanford University
→ LinkedIn Profile → tktan@ieee.org

Dr. Tan brings over 35 years of global leadership spanning Silicon Valley start-up exits, Fortune 500 consulting, and frontier AI research. Former CTO at Accenture, with roles at Cisco, NXP, FINRA, Johns Hopkins Medicine, and IMDA.

His research sits at the intersection of quantitative methodology and applied AI — using machine learning, SEM, and data science to tackle real-world management problems. He holds multiple wireless patents and has authored several books on AI, fintech, and data analytics.

This programme reflects his conviction that the next generation of doctoral researchers must be fluent in both classical statistics and AI-native methods.
